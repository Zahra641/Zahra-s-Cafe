origin & history of cafe

The term “café”, which is widely used to describe a restaurant that primarily serves coffee and snacks, has a rich history that dates back to the 15th century. The origin of the café, or coffee house, can be traced back to modern-day Somalia, Ethiopia, and Yemen, where coffee was already known in the 15th century. It was in Yemen that coffee seeds were first roasted and brewed in a manner similar to how it is now prepared for drinking. The coffee beans were procured from the Ethiopian Highlands via coastal Somali intermediaries and cultivated in Yemen.

Menu

Café menus typically focus on lighter fare and beverages compared to other types of restaurants. They often offer a variety of coffee and tea options, pastries, sandwiches, and salads. Some may also serve breakfast items throughout the day. The emphasis is on casual, quick meals and snacks that can be enjoyed alongside a hot drink, often in a relaxed, cozy atmosphere.

Source:About the menu.

What is a cafe

A café, also known as a coffeehouse or coffee shop, is a type of establishment that primarily serves coffee and tea, along with light refreshments such as baked goods or snacks. The term "café" originates from the French word for "coffee". These establishments are known for their casual social environment where people engage in various activities such as reading, studying, or discussing current events. They are also places where information can be exchanged.
